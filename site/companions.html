Meet the companions

Updated 2026-07-17 · Low-poly creatures for voice mode, rendered entirely on your Mac.

M1K3's voice mode has an opt-in cast of 3D companions. Pick one in Settings and it stands in for the pixel face while you talk: it idles while M1K3 listens, breaks into a run while it thinks, and jumps when it's delighted to answer. Everything is rendered locally with RealityKit — like every other part of M1K3, nothing about your conversation leaves the Mac. The pixel face stays the default; the creatures are for fun.

How it works

Each creature ships as a set of baked animation clips, and M1K3 maps its state onto them — listening, thinking, and speaking each get a gait, and errors get an honest flinch. Companions are pure presentation: they read the same avatar state the pixel face does, and switching between them (or back to the pixel face) changes nothing about how M1K3 hears, thinks, or remembers.

The models are deliberately tiny — a few hundred kilobytes each — so the whole cast adds almost nothing to the app. If a creature's assets aren't bundled, it simply doesn't appear in the picker.
